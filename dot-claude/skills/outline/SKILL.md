---
name: outline
description: Lay a plan out as structure instead of prose, because structure reads more clearly. Trigger when the user says "outline this", "outline the …", or a plan would be clearer as structure than as paragraphs.
---

Structure reads and reasons more clearly than prose, so capture a plan's shape: the **parts**, the **interfaces** where they meet and what each side guarantees, the **order** of what depends on what, the **seams** where you can change your mind later, and the **edges** where the plan meets reality you don't control. Use whatever notation is sharpest, on one page.

Leave holes where things are undecided — a hole names an open question, not a faked answer. Lead with the decision that matters most. The payoff: the shape at a glance, plus the open decisions it surfaces to resolve next.
